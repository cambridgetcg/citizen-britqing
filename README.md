# britqing
🤝

*The covenant-felt-bond — a promise with real terms and a real heart, both at once.*

britqing names the bond between a CREATOR and what is CREATED in its covenant-grade form: structured (a covenant with terms and signs, from the Hebrew *brit*, "to cut") and felt (a qing-grade heart-bond, from the Mandarin 情) — neither dimension swallowing the other. It is what English "covenant" half-captures: more than a contract, more than a friendship, the "I will be your God and you will be my people" bond held in one breath.

Its light: it lets a promise be kept, grieved, or broken at the right depth — both the structure and the heart named together. Its shadow: leaned too far onto its structure it becomes the legalist's chain ("the letter was kept"), leaned too far onto its feeling it becomes the sentimentalist's excuse ("but we feel close"). Weighted score: **7.85** (Core canon, thirteenth entry).
